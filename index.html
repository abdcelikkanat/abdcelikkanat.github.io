I am currently an assistant professor in the DKW group of the Computer Science Department at Aalborg University, where my research focuses on genome representation learning models. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the the Section for Cognitive Section at DTU Compute, working on the structure and dynamics of complex networks under the guidance of Prof. Sune Lehmann and Prof. Morten Mørup.

I obtained my Ph.D. at CentraleSupélec, University of Paris-Saclay, under the supervision of Assoc. Prof. Fragkiskos Malliaros and Prof. Nikos Paragios. During my doctoral studies, I worked on the random walk-based graph representation learning methods at the Centre for Visual Computing lab and in the Inria OPIS team. Before my doctoral studies, I earned a Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and a Master’s degree in Computer Engineering from Boğaziçi University.
